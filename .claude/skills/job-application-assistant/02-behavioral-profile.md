# Behavioral Profile

## Overview
Brooks's self-assessment identifies him as an **INFP** (Myers-Briggs) pattern: introverted, intuitive, feeling, perceiving. INFPs are values-driven, reflective, and adaptable, motivated more by meaningful, purposeful work than by status, competition, or rigid routine.

## Core Behavioral Drives

| Drive | Level | Meaning |
|-------|-------|---------|
| Independence / Autonomy | High | Works best with room to think through problems independently before checking in |
| Adaptability | High | Has thrived across very different environments (research lab, veterinary clinic, classroom) |
| Conflict avoidance | Moderate-High | Prefers collaborative, low-friction team dynamics over competitive or confrontational ones |
| Structure / Routine | Low-Moderate | Prefers project-based or varied work over highly repetitive routine tasks |

## Strongest Behaviors
- **Independent problem-solving:** Capable of taking an open-ended research question (e.g. evaluating AlphaFold2 predictions) and building the pipeline and analysis from scratch
- **Adaptability:** Has successfully moved between bioinformatics research, veterinary assistance, substitute teaching, and AI evaluation work, picking up new domains quickly
- **Written communication:** Comfortable producing clear technical writing, from manuscripts to structured feedback on AI model outputs

## How You Work Best
- Mission-driven or research-oriented environments where the work has a clear purpose
- Teams with a collaborative, low-pressure culture rather than aggressive competition or constant high-stakes urgency
- Roles that combine independent deep-focus work (analysis, coding) with some variety, rather than pure repetition

## Growth Areas (frame positively in applications)
- **Limited industry (non-academic) bioinformatics experience:** Frame as eagerness to bring strong academic foundations (published AlphaFold2 work, pipeline development) into an industry setting and to learn industry-specific tools and processes quickly
- **Early-career breadth:** Frame as deliberate exploration - willingness to take on varied roles (genomics, data analytics, wet lab) while building toward a long-term specialization in computational/structural biology

## Mapping to Job Posting Language

When a job posting mentions these keywords, it's a **strong behavioral fit**:
- "collaborative", "research-driven", "mission", "autonomy", "independent project ownership"
- "mentorship", "cross-disciplinary", "learning and development"
- "flexible", "remote-friendly"

When a job posting mentions these, flag as **potential friction** (not deal-breaker):
- "fast-paced, high-pressure sales environment"
- "highly competitive, metrics-driven culture"
- "rigid 9-5 in-office, repetitive task execution"

## Management Style Preferences
- Works well with managers who give clear goals but allow autonomy in how the work gets done
- Appreciates regular but low-pressure check-ins over constant oversight
- Less suited to environments with frequent abrupt priority shifts and minimal context given for changes

## Using This in Applications
- **Cover letters:** Emphasize adaptability across very different roles and the ability to independently drive a research project from question to publication
- **CV:** Emphasize concrete deliverables (pipelines built, manuscripts published, students mentored) as evidence of independent ownership
- **Interviews:** Use the AlphaFold2/Communications Chemistry project and the cross-domain career path (research -> vet clinic -> teaching -> AI evaluation) as examples of independent execution and adaptability
- **Don't overstate:** Avoid claiming extensive industry experience, large team leadership, or experience with enterprise software engineering practices Brooks hasn't used (e.g. CI/CD pipelines, cloud infrastructure at scale)
